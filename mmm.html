Problem

Climate change brings extreme weather hazards like wildfires, hurricanes, thunderstorms, and more. These hazards not only affect residents in local areas but also impact tourists‚Äô travel schedules. Tourists are usually new to the areas and are not aware of how to navigate such hazards. Yet, there are no useful tools to help tourists plan their trip in conjunction with weather hazards.


Lean Canvas & Competitive Analysis for Brainstorming

As a team, we utilized a lean canvas to brainstorm the business scenario and user needs to solve this problem. It also includes a competitive analysis. After several discussions, we decided to make a mobile app because it makes it easier for tourists to access weather information.
We also narrowed down the app‚Äôs features:

(1) Show the predicted climate emergency of the user‚Äôs trip at a selected location in a time frame
(2) Recommend nearby spots that are safe to travel to
(3) Log the trip results
(4) Educate our users on climate change and how to tackle it


Research

We utilized literature search as our background research method. Through this traditional yet effective method, we collected data about climate change. We gained insights about ranking of causes that contribute most to climate change, news articles about climate change and tourism, government data about climate change, and a world map about global warming.

Takeaways:

• Climate change harms tourism in many ways.
• Global warming lengthens the period of summer that is unpleasantly hot in beach resort regions.
• Global warming shortens ski seasons in mountain retreats.
• Increasing water scarcity in warmer regions becomes a potential for conflict between locals and the resource-intensive tourism industry.
• Wildfire is one of the most common natural disasters caused by climate change, and it affects tourists more directly than other disasters.

Source: https://time.com/6090722/tourism-climate/

Wireframe Usability Testing

Low-fidelity wireframes; we wanted to have an "alert" feature at first.

Our group conducted a usability testing session with another classmate who was really into traveling. During our in-person meeting at Starbucks, our team assigned him three tasks:

(1) Look up Irvine and find the weather hazard during a time frame
(2) If the weather hazard is severe in the area, look for nearby place to travel
(3) Find and read climate change news

Overall, the user found it easy to complete the tasks, without much hesitation. The user said the app flow was intuitive enough for him to locate major functionalities. Yet, he pointed out that the ‚ÄúCall 911 for help‚Äù functionality does not seem necessary as the app is about planning the trip beforehand rather than evacuating a dangerous area. For that reason, we decided to remove the feature in our high-fidelity prototype.

Design System

Design system for Good Weather

We incorporated colors and elements that represent weather to set up our design system. For example, I mixed common symbols that represent location and weather to design the logo, by adding a sky blue ring and yellow dots to a map marker to characterize clouds and the sun.

Key Features

Home

Home page greets the users with the line: ‚ÄúWelcome, Traveler!‚Äù From our home page, users can see a short summary of the current location‚Äôs weather, which includes the location name, highest weather hazard possibility, weather condition as of now, temperature, and overall safety of the location (smile or frown icon). Users can also see some of the recent climate change news here.

Plan Your Trip

Using this feature, users can see if a location is safe enough to travel during their chosen time frame. They can see the location‚Äôs weather hazards, temperature, weather, humidity, and recommended nearby areas that are safe to travel. They can also read more information about the weather hazard of the location, about what it is exactly and what can we do to prevent it.

Find safer area to travel & log trip

Users can navigate safer areas with less or no weather hazard possibilities, and once they choose where to travel, they can log their trip information.

My Trip Summary

Users can view their logged trip summaries. These summaries include location name, trip timeline, weather conditions during the trip, and chances of weather hazards.

Climate Change News

This page displays recent news about climate change. This feature gives users a sense of the seriousness of climate change and encourages them to take actions.
